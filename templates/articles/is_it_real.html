Climate Change - is it real?

"Scientific evidence for warming of the climate system is unequivocal." - IPCC on Climate Change.

Earths climate fluctuates throughout history. In the past 650,000 years there have been seven cycles of glaciers advancing and retreating again. This causes people to use this as an argument for what is happening to our planet now, and why we shouldn't worry.

What's different now?

The big difference right now is that the latest rise in CO2 levels is a direct result of the actions of humans in the modern era. Levels are currently way higher than they've ever been over many millenia. It is undeniable that humans have caused the warmth of the atmosphere, ocean, and land and cause rapid changes to the planet's ecosystems.

What is some of the evidence?

  • Global Temperature Rising - The planet's average surface temperature has risen ~1.18 degrees Celsius since the 19th century. This change is largely driven by increased CO2 emmissions in the atmosphere and other human activities. The majority of this warming occurred in the last 40 years with 2016 and 2020 being tied as the warmest years on record.
  • Sea Level Rising - Global sea levels have risen 20 centimeters in the last 100 years. However, the rate of that in the last two decased is almost double that of the last 100 years and is still accelerating every year.
  • Extreme Weather - The number of record-breaking high temperature related events in the US has been increasing. During this, the number of record-breaking low temperature events has been decreasing. This shows the planets rising temperature is having an effect on the planet. An example of this would be the large increase in intense rainfall recordied in the US since 1950.

What can we do?

Major changes need to come from the governments and businesses around the world. Countries have agreed to tackle this problem together and try to keep global warming to 1.5C. There has been plans to reduce Carbon emissions by 2050. Experts say that this is achievable and would require the government and individuals to make major changes.
